<strong><strong><strong><span class="dcap">H</span><strong>ow to Fix Hibernate problems on Windows 10?</strong></strong></strong></strong> Hibernate is a power mode that works similar to the sleep mode, but it saves the state of your device into the hard drive, instead of keeping apps running on memory. Using hibernation, you can completely power off your computer, and later resume exactly where you left off.

In this article, we describe some<strong> simple steps to resolve problems using the Hibernate option on Windows 10.</strong>
<h2 id="1">Fix hibernation using a different power plan:</h2>
Follow the below steps to fix hibernation using different power plans in simple steps.

<strong>Resetting power plan:</strong>
<ol>
 	<li>Go to start menu and in the search column type as <strong>Command Prompt.</strong></li>
 	<li>Right-click on the Command Prompt and select <strong>Run as administrator.</strong></li>
 	<li>To reset your power plan copy and paste the below command and press <strong><strong>Enter.</strong></strong>
<pre><code>powercfg –restoredefaultschemes</code></pre>

</ol>
<strong>Create a new power plan:</strong>
<ol>
 	<li>Go to the start menu.</li>
 	<li>In the search column, type as <strong>Control Panel</strong>.

 	<li>Then, click on <strong>Hardware and Sound</strong>.

[caption id="attachment_1219" align="aligncenter" width="842"]<img class="wp-image-1219 size-full" src="https://windowscrazy.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Screenshot_.png" alt="Control panel" width="842" height="608" /> Control panel[/caption]</li>
 	<li>Select on <strong>Power Options</strong>.

 	<li>In the left pane, click the <strong>Create a power plan</strong> link.

<h2 id="2"> Fix hibernation using Power Troubleshooter:</h2>
Follow the below steps to fix hibernation using Power Troubleshooter in simple steps.
<ol>
 	<li>Use the shortcut <strong>Windows +I</strong> to open Settings.</li>
 	<li>Select <strong>Update &amp; Security</strong>.</li>
 	<li>In the left pane, click on <strong>Troubleshoot</strong>.</li>
 	<li>Then, select the <strong>Power</strong> option in the Troubleshoot.</li>
 	<li>Click the <strong>Run the troubleshooter</strong> button.

<h2 id="3">Fix hibernation using disabling Fast Startup:</h2>
Follow the below steps to fix hibernation using disabling Fast Startup in simple steps.
<ol>
 	<li>Go to the control panel.</li>
 	<li>Select  <strong>Hardware and Sound</strong> option and then, click on <strong>Power Options</strong>.</li>
 	<li>In the left pane, click the <strong>Choose what the power buttons do</strong> link.

[caption id="attachment_1224" align="aligncenter" width="842"]<img class="wp-image-1224 size-full" src="https://windowscrazy.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Screenshot_5-5.png" alt="Power Options" width="842" height="614" /> Power Options[/caption]</li>
 	<li>Now, click the <strong>Change settings that are currently unavailable</strong> link.</li>
 	<li>Then, uncheck the  <strong>Turn on fast startup</strong> option.

[caption id="attachment_1225" align="aligncenter" width="1284"]<img class="wp-image-1225 size-full" src="https://windowscrazy.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Screenshot_6-3.png" alt="Check out" width="1284" height="647" /> Check out[/caption]</li>
 	<li>Finally, click the <strong>Save Changes</strong> button.</li>
 	<li>After completion, restart your computer.</li>
</ol>
